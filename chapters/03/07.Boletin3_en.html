3.7.1. Basic exercises#

[1] Sea \(f\) la función dada por \(f(x)=5x^2.\)

  1. Use the definition of the derivative to show that \(f'(x)=10x.\)

  2. Calculate the equation of the tangent line to the graph of \(f\) in \(\displaystyle{x=\frac12}.\)

[2] Sea \(f : \mathbb{R} \longrightarrow \mathbb{R}\) dada por:

\[\begin{split} f(x) = \begin{cases} 2x - 1 & \quad \text{si } x \leq -1 \\ ( x+1)^3 + 2x & \quad \text{si } x > -1 \end{cases} \end{split}\]

Find out if it is differentiable at \(x = -1\).

[3] Let \(f\) be the function given by

\[\begin{split} f(x)= \begin{cases} \displaystyle{x+x^2 \sin\left(\frac 1x\right)} & \text{ si } x\neq 0 \\ 0 & \text{ si } x = 0 \end{cases} \end{split}\]

  1. Obtain the expression of \(f'(x)\) for \(x\neq 0\).

  2. Calculate \(f' (0)\) using the definition of derivative; that is, as a limit of the incremental quotient.

  3. Check that the value obtained in section [3.2] does not coincide with the result of replacing \(x\) with zero in the expression found in [3.1]. Reason why these different results occur.

[4] Consider the function \(f\) given by:

\[\begin{split} f(x) = \begin{cases} \displaystyle{\frac{1-e^{-x}}{x} } & \quad \text{si } x \neq 0 \\ 1 & \quad \text{si } x = 0 \end{cases} \end{split}\]

  1. Calculate \(f'(x)\) at all points where it exists.

  2. Calcula \(\displaystyle{\lim _{x\rightarrow -\infty}{f(x)}}\), \(\displaystyle{\lim _{x\rightarrow \infty}{f(x)}}\) y el conjunto \(\textrm{Im}(f)\).

[5] Write the Newton-Raphson method to approximate \(\sqrt[3]{2}\) starting from \(x_0 = 1\). Calculate the first two iterants.

[6] To approximate the value of the solution of the equation \(x^3+2x-2=0,\) in the interval \([-2,\, 2],\) we are going to use the Newton-Raphson method.

  1. State the Newton-Raphson algorithm for this case.

  2. Calculate the first two iterations using that algorithm. To do this, use the value \(x_0=0\) as an initial approximation.

[7] Let \(f(x) = \displaystyle \arctan \left( \frac{\sin (x)}{1 + \cos (x)} \right )\). Calculate the tangent line to the graph of \(f\) at \(x = 0\).

[8] Let the function \(f\) be given by \(f(x) = (4x+1)^{(2+ \sin (x^2))}\). Calculate its derivative at any point.

[9] If we consider the function \(f:\mathbb{R}\rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) given by

\[\begin{split} f(x)=\displaystyle{\left\{ \begin{array}{ccl} judisplaystyle{yufrac{yusin^2(zx)}{zx^2}} & si & hyunea 0 yuu \\ \displaystyle{\frac13 }& si & x=0 \end{array} \right. }, \end{split}\]

choose the correct option:

  1. \(\displaystyle\lim _{x\rightarrow 0}f(x)=\frac{1}{3} \).

  2. Cannot calculate \(\displaystyle{\lim _{x\rightarrow 0}{f(x)}}\).

  3. \(\displaystyle{\lim _{x\rightarrow 0}{f(x)}}=3\).

  4. It is continuous in \(\mathbb{R}\)

[10] Calculate the values ​​of \(a\) and \(b\) so that

\[ \displaystyle{\lim_{x\rightarrow 0}\frac{ax^2+bx+1-e^{2x}}{\sin(x^2)}=1} \]

[11] Let the function \(f\) be given by \(\displaystyle f(x) = \frac{1}{2} x |x|\). What is the class of \(f\)?

[12] We consider the function \(f\) given by

\[\begin{split} f(x) = \begin{cases} \sin (x) \, \qquad \text{ si } x \in (-\infty,0) \\ \arctan (x) \,\qquad \text{ si } x \in [0,1] \\ \pi x^3 / 4 \, \qquad \text{ si } x \in (1,+\infty) \end{cases} \end{split}\]

  1. Sketch the graphical representation of \(f\).

  2. Determine the class of \(f\) in \(\mathbb{R}\).

  3. Calculate, if possible, the tangent line to \(f\) at the point \(x_0 = 0\).

  4. Determine a set of real numbers in which \(f\) is of class infinite.

[13] Let \(f\) be the function given by

\[\begin{split} f(x) = \begin{cases} \sqrt{x} + 1 & \quad \text{si } x \in (0,1) \\ \alpha x^2 + \beta x + 1 & \quad \text{si } x \in [1,2) \end{cases} \end{split}\]

  1. Determine \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) so that \(f\) is differentiable in \((0,2)\).

  2. What conditions must \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) satisfy so that \(f\) has a relative minimum at the point \(x=1\)?

[14] Calculates the relative and absolute extremes if they exist, of the function defined in the intervalo \(\left [ -\frac{1}{2} , \frac{3}{2} \right ]\) por \(f(x) = x^2 - 2|x| + 2\).

[15] Consider the function \(f:\mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) given by \(\displaystyle{f(x)=\frac{|x|}{e^{x-1}}}\).

  1. Study the continuity and differentiability of the function.

  2. Sketch the graph of \(f\). To do this, it calculates their relative extremes, inflection points and asymptotes. Also determine its interval(s) of concavity and/or convexity.

  3. Determine the absolute extremes of \(f\).

[16] Show that the equation \(x^4-4\,x^3-1=0\) has a single root in the interval \([4,5]\). Pose the Newton-Raphson method to solve the equation from the previous section. Starting from \(x_0=4\), obtain the approximation \(x_1\) by hand and the approximation \(x_7\) using Python.

[17] On what interval is the function \(f\) given by \(f(x)=x^3e^x\) increasing? Is it concave or convex in that interval? Sketch your graph.

[18] Find the condition that \(\lambda\) must satisfy for the polynomial \(x^4 + x^3 + \lambda x^2\) to be concave on some interval. Determine that interval based on \(\lambda\).

[19] Find a cubic polynomial without local extrema but with an inflection point and a horizontal tangent at \(P=(1,\, 20)\).

Sketch the graph of a function whose slope is always positive and decreasing. Do you know of any elementary function that is an example of this situation?

[21] A rectangle whose base is on the abscissa axis has its two upper vertices in the parabola \(y = 12 - x^2\). What is the largest area that this rectangle can have? Indicate its dimensions.

[22] Knowing that \(p(x)=5+(x+1)^2+3(x+1)^3\) is the third-order Taylor polynomial centered at \(x_0=-1\) of a function \(f,\) calculate \(f(-1),\) \(f^\prime (-1)\) and \(f^{\prime \prime}(-1).\) Justify your answer.

[23] We consider the function \(f\) defined by \(f(x)=\ln(1+x)\).

  1. Calculate the McLaurin polynomial (i.e., the Taylor polynomial with \(x_0=0\)) of order two relative to \(f\).

  2. Use this polynomial to approximate the value of \(\ln(1.1)\), limiting the error made.

[24] Construct the first-order Taylor polynomial, \(p\), for the function \(g(x) = \sin (x)\), centered at the point \(x_0 = \pi/2\).

Now consider the function \(f\) defined as follows:

\[\begin{split} f(x) = \begin{cases} \sin (x) \quad & \quad \quad \text{si } x \leq \pi/2 \\ p(x) \quad & \quad \quad \text{si } x > \pi/2 \end{cases} \end{split}\]

\(p\) being the polynomial constructed in the previous section. What is the class of \(f\) in \(\mathbb{R}\)?

[26] Let the function given by

\[\begin{split} \begin{array}{cccl} f: & \mathcal{D}om(f) \subset \mathbb{R} & \longrightarrow & \mathbb{R} \\ & x & \rightsquigarrow & f(x) = \dfrac{x^2}{e^x} \end{array} \end{split}\]

3.7.2. Complementary exercises#

[1 C] If the tangent line to the graph of the function \(y = f(x)\) at the point \((4,3)\) passes through the point \((0,2)\), calculates the value of the function \(f\) and its derivative at the point whose abscissa is \(x = 4\).

[2 C] Approximate the solution of \(x^3 - x - 1 = 0\) in \([1.3,\, 1.4]\) using the Newton-Raphson method, starting from \(x_0 = 1\) and performing three iterations.

[3 C] Find the numbers \(a\) and \(b\) such that \(\,\,\displaystyle{\lim _{x\rightarrow 0}{\frac{\sqrt{ax+b}-2}{x}}=1}. \)

[4 C] Calculate the limit:

\[ \displaystyle{\lim_{x\rightarrow 0} \dfrac{(1+x)^{\frac{1}{x}} - e}{x}.} \]

[5 C] We consider the function \(f: \mathbb{R} \longrightarrow \mathbb{R}\) given by:

\[\begin{split} f(x)= \begin{cases} \displaystyle{\dfrac{1-\cos (x)}{x^2}} & \text{ si } x\neq 0 \\ \lambda & \text{ si } x = 0 \end{cases} \end{split}\]

  1. Calculate the value of \(\lambda\) for which \(f\) is differentiable at \(x=0\).

  2. Calculate \(f^{\prime \prime}(0)\) for the value of \(\lambda\) found in the previous section.

[6 C] Let \(f:\mathbb{R}\to \mathbb{R}\) defined as \(f(x) = \begin{cases} \displaystyle 1 - \frac{x^2}{2} & \text{ si } x < 0 \\ \cos (x) & \text{ si } x \geq 0 \end{cases} \).
How many times is \(f\) differentiable at \(x_{0}=0\)?

[7 C] Check that the function \(F\), given by \(F(x) = \dfrac{1}{4} x^2 - \ln x\), has a relative minimum, \(x_{\min}\), in the interval \( (1.3)\). Use the dichotomy algorithm, starting from \(a = 1\) and \(b = 3\), to approximate \(x_{\min}\) with an error less than \(\frac{1}{7}\).

[8 C] We consider the equation , \(x e^{-x} = e^{-3}\).

  1. Check that you have exactly two solutions in \(\mathbb{R}\).

  2. Establish the Newton-Raphson method starting from a point in the interval \([2,5]\). Calculate \(x_2\) from \(x_0 = 2\).

[9 C] We wish to construct an arc that describes the curve given by \(f(x) = \sqrt{1-x^2}\). To facilitate construction, it is proposed to approximate said function by a second-order Taylor polynomial centered on \(a = 0\). Calculate said polynomial and approximate \(f(1)\).

[10 C] During coughing, the diameter of the trachea decreases. The velocity \(v\) of the air in the trachea during cough is related to the radius, \(r,\) by the equation:

\[ v = Ar^2 (r_0-r) \quad , \qquad A > 0 \]

where \(r_0\) is the radius in the relaxed state.

  1. Find the radius of the trachea when the speed is maximum, as well as this speed.

  2. Justifies the existence of a minimum. calculate it

[11 C] Find the radius and height of a cylindrical soda can. \(33\) cm\(^3\) that minimizes the amount of material used for its construction (assume that the thickness of the material used is uniform throughout the can and negligible).

[12 C] Let the real function \(f\) be given by:

\[\begin{split} f(x) = \begin{cases} \frac{\sin( x)}{x} - 1 \quad and \quad \quad \text{si } x < 0 \\ & \\ x^3 e^{-x^2} \quad & \quad \quad \text{si } x \geq 0 \, . \end{cases} \end{split}\]

  1. Study the differentiability of \(f\) in \(\mathbb{R}\).

  2. Calculate the second-order Taylor polynomial of the function \(f\) in the neighborhood of \(x_0 = 1\).

  3. Reasonably determine the absolute extremes of \(f\) in \([0,+\infty)\).

[13 C] Let the function \(f\) be given by:

\[\begin{split} f(x) = \begin{cases} \dfrac{\sin (x)}{x} \, & \quad \quad \text{si } x < 0 \\ 2 - \cos(x) \, & \quad \quad \text{si } x \geq 0 \, . \end{cases} \end{split}\]

  1. Study the continuity of \(f\) in \(\mathbb{R}\).
